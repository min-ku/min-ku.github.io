Hi, I'm Minku Kim. I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering and Applied Mechanics (MEAM) with a concentration of Mechatronics and Robotic Systems at the University of Pennsylvania. I am interested in working at the intersection of control theory, machine learning and computer vision.

More specifically, in integrating learning-based methods including Reinforcement learning with model-based approaches and rich sensory feedback to enhance robotic adaptability in complex tasks and unpredictable environments. My current research centers on developing vision-based hierarchical locomotion controllers for navigating unstructured terrains, with the underactuated bipedal robot Cassie in the Dynamic Autonomy and Intelligent Robotics Lab (DAIR) Lab advised by Professor Michael Posa and co-advised by Professor Pratik Chaudhari.

Before joining Penn, I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering at Chung-Ang University, where I conducted research on applying Computer Vision and learning-based methods to improve robustness and adaptability in mechanical systems such as UAVs, Electric Vehicle (EV) reducers and wearable devices.
